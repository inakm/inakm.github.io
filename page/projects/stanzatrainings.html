Communication is generally confident with occasional hesitation and repetition. With targeted coaching on deeper need discovery and a tighter value-before-price script, they should perform well in a student accommodation sales role.

Key Strengths

  • Naturally integrates services (app, hygiene, laundry) into value
  • Stays patient and empathetic with first-time student concerns
  • Holds ground on price without knee-jerk discounting
  • Clear articulation of managed, standardized brand vs local PGs

Areas for Improvement

  • Deepen discovery: ask about distance to college, study routine, stay duration, privacy, safety priorities, decision makers, and must-haves
  • Tighten value-before-price: use a 2-3 point recap and a single anchor line before quoting numbers
  • Increase pricing clarity: show total monthly estimate with inclusions, deposits, notice period, and likely variability (electricity, laundry overages)
  • Sharpen differentiators with proof points: ticket SLA, response times, guard coverage, CCTV stats, hygiene audit cadence, parent testimonials
  • Reduce fillers and code-mixing; use concise headlines and close each phase with a brief summary and a check for alignment

Rapport Building

  • Warm greeting, welcomed the student, offered a seat, invited discussion of needs; acknowledged first-time-away nervousness
  • Friendly tone, attempted to put customer at ease, did not crowd; initiated conversation promptly
  • Could personalize opener more and mirror nervous energy with slower pacing; reduce filler words and code-mixing for clarity
  • Did not ask small comfort questions (timing of visit, quick water/tea offer); missed light rapport cues about college/course/location

Brand Introduction

  • Positioned Stanza Living as a global, standardized brand with 9+ years' experience; highlighted app, hygiene, security, and multi-city presence
  • Clear differentiators (standardization, safety, managed operations, app-based management); confidence about scale and credibility
  • Messaging felt scattered and repetitive; needs a tighter 2?3 point value story with crisp phrasing
  • No proof points like SLAs, response times, parent reviews, or safety stats; lacked quick contrast story vs local PGs

Need Identification

  • Asked about needs and budget; categorized room types (single/double/triple) and invited the budget to narrow options
  • Showed patience; did not rush straight to a room sell; acknowledged first-time concerns
  • Limited open-ended probing (no questions on college distance, timing, study schedule, cleanliness sensitivity, food preferences, safety priorities, roommate preferences, stay duration)

Product Pitch Quality
